The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star located at the center of the Solar System.
Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has no natural moons.
Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets in the Solar System.
Earth is the only planet known to support liquid surface water.
Mars has the largest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons.
Jupiter is the largest planet and has a prominent storm called the Great Red Spot.
Saturn is known for its extensive ring system made mostly of ice particles.
Uranus has an axial tilt that makes it appear to roll along its orbit.
Neptune was discovered through mathematical prediction before it was observed.
The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains the Solar System.
