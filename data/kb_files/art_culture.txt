The Museum of Modern Art in New York focuses on contemporary art from the late 19th century onward.
The Louvre Museum in Paris houses the painting known as the Mona Lisa.
The Tate Modern is a major contemporary art museum in London and occupies a former power station.
The Venice Biennale is an international art exhibition held every two years.
The Uffizi Gallery in Florence is known for Renaissance art collections.
The Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York spans multiple historical periods and cultures.
The Guggenheim Museum in Bilbao is known for its titanium-clad building designed by Frank Gehry.
The British Museum in London holds extensive collections from ancient civilizations.
The Hermitage Museum in Saint Petersburg is among the largest museums in the world.
The Prado Museum in Madrid is noted for European masterworks from the 12th to the early 20th century.
