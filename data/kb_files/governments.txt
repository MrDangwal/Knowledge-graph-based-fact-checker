A democracy is a system of government in which citizens can vote for representatives.
A constitution defines the structure and powers of a government.
A federal system divides power between a central authority and regional units.
A parliamentary system typically has a prime minister as head of government.
A republic is a form of government without a monarch.
A bicameral legislature has two chambers or houses.
An executive branch enforces laws passed by the legislature.
The judicial branch interprets laws and adjudicates disputes.
Sovereignty refers to supreme authority within a territory.
The rule of law means that laws apply equally to all individuals.
