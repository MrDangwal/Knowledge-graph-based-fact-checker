Cells are the basic structural and functional units of living organisms.
DNA carries genetic information in most living organisms.
Photosynthesis converts carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen using sunlight.
Mitochondria are organelles often described as the powerhouses of the cell.
Proteins are made from amino acids linked by peptide bonds.
The human brain is part of the central nervous system.
The circulatory system transports blood and nutrients throughout the body.
Viruses require host cells to replicate.
Bacteria are single-celled microorganisms found in diverse environments.
Evolution by natural selection explains how populations change over time.
