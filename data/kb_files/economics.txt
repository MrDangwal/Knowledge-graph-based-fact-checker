Inflation refers to a sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services.
A central bank typically sets monetary policy to influence interest rates and inflation.
Gross domestic product (GDP) measures the total value of goods and services produced within a country.
Unemployment rates track the share of the labor force that is without work but seeking employment.
Supply and demand are basic economic forces that influence prices.
A recession is commonly defined as a significant decline in economic activity over multiple months.
Fiscal policy involves government spending and taxation decisions.
Trade deficits occur when a country imports more goods and services than it exports.
Exchange rates determine the value of one currency relative to another.
Productivity measures output per unit of input, such as labor hours.
